In this section of the website I provide graphs (provided by the department of public health dashboard) that summarise the local data. The first graph is a traditional scatter plot of number of cases (y-axis) plotted against time (x-axis). There are five graphs on one plot. The red points represent the total (cumulative) number of people who have been diagnosed with COVID-19 in Malta. The yellow points represent the total number of people who have recovered while the blue dots represent the total number of people who have, unfortunately, died of COVID-19. The purple points represent the "Active Cases" that is the total number of cases at the moment (which is the total number of cases minus the number of recoveries and deaths).

The next graph shows the age distribution of the data. The third graph classifies the types of cases into local or imported and the last graph indicates the number of new cases per day.


To be able to properly interpret the age distribution graph above, it is useful to have the general population age distribution (left side). The graph to the right gives an indication of how well Malta is doing in terms of what we would expect to see if the virus was spreading unchecked in an infinite population. The red line is the "expected growth" while the blue line is the "actual growth." This is a bit of a tricky graph but luckily, minute physics has provided a great video with an in-depth explanation of its interpretation. The video can be found on the educational section of this site.

All of the above raw data that were used to produce these graphs can be found on this Google sheet that is updated regularly.
