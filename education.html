SARS-Cov-2

The first thing to get out of the way is the name! Coronavirus, novel coronavirus, SARS-Cov-2 and COVID-19, what are all these names? Are they the same thing? What are the differences. All the terms are slightly differnt. Coronavirus is a general name for a family (or group) of viruses. There are many coronaviruses around one of the new (novel) coronavirus that the The International Committee on Taxonomy of Viruses (basically the group of people that officially names viruses) called SARS-Cov-2. See Nature paper.
